AI in Waste Genie

We are experimenting and evaluating Expert, Crowdsourcing and AI approaches to create content, specifically, how LLM can be capitalized in creating sustainability learning content, and is the method sustainable?

We experimented with open source LLM applications, ChatGPT 3.5 Turbo, meta's LLaMA2, etc. The results are either not correct or too generic, and most importantly, not location specific. The straightforward solution is to integrate all the legal documents (legislative regulations about waste) into the LLM, however, it also indicates a severe hallucination of the model.

We also experimented engineering Environmental Legislative-guided LLM to help extract relevant information. Please refer to our IEEE CAI'25 & ACM COMPASS'25 papers.

LLM Comparison: Meta LLaMA 2 vs ChatGPT 3.5 Turbo - demonstrating challenges with location-specific waste guidance

Environmental Legislative-guided LLM Pipeline: From original legislation to educational content with AI evaluation

Lessons Learned

Key insights from our sustainability education research

Gamification in Informal Learning

Our research has revealed important insights about implementing gamification elements in informal sustainability education settings. Through extensive user studies and platform analytics, we've discovered key principles for effective gamification design.

Gamification Implementation: User interface examples and engagement metrics

Read & Interact First, Publish and Contribute Later

Through our Waste Genie platform research, we discovered that encouraging users to "Read & Interact First, Publish and Contribute Later" leads to higher quality contributions and better learning outcomes. This approach allows users to familiarize themselves with the platform's content and community standards before actively contributing, resulting in more thoughtful and valuable user-generated content.

Theory of Planned Behavior Modeled EdTech in Waste Management Learning

We applied Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB) to model the users' behavior intention in waste management. The underlying assumption is that we all want to practice sustainable behaviors for a better tomorrow (i.e. proper waste sorting; recycle; reduce trash; etc.), but there is a gap between intention and actual behavior.

Theory of Planned Behavior Model: Analyzing behavioral intention in waste management practices

Results

There are significant relations between Self-efficacy and Literacy & Literacy and Attitude.

Lesson Learned

Waste management knowledge (literacy) indeed plays a crucial role in the mix of self-believing one can be part of the environmental crusade.

Practical Solution to Engineer Balanced Waste Dataset

Why are the existing datasets not enough?

• Internet crawled images can be noisy. (i.e. an image labeled as straw often is not just a straw, it usually comes with a cup, a hand and a place, etc.)

• Small objects can be hard to train.

In our previously deployed model (assembled upon open data sets, and some additions from our efforts), there is a total 55043 images across 24 categories.

Dataset Distribution: Imbalanced waste classification data across 24 categories

Approaches to address imbalanced data

Generative AI Solutions: Approaches to balance waste classification datasets

Results

  • AI-boosted image categories had higher entropy.
  • AI-infused images improved the image diversity.
  • Generative-AI is more prominent in improving model accuracy with biased or imbalanced datasets.
  • Cost-efficiency of the boosted data and model accuracy can be achieved.
